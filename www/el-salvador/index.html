In 2001, El Salvador was hit with two large earthquakes that killed more than 1,000 people and damaged one in five homes. The devastation was so vast, President George W. Bush granted some Salvadorans living in the United States temporary protected status (TPS). This move shielded them from deportation and allowed them to work here legally.

The Trump administration reversed that decision this week, saying the more than 200,000 people from the Central American nation will have their temporary residency permits cancelled. After nearly 17 years, the Department of Homeland Security said in a statement that “the original conditions caused by the 2001 earthquakes no longer exist.”

Signed into law by President George H. W. Bush in 1990, the U.S. has extended the TPS program several times since 2001. Immigrant advocates and the El Salvadoran government had pleaded for another extension.
