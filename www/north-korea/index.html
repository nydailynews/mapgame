Oh, North Korea.

At the start of 2018 it was the battle of words, when President Trump taunted North Korean Supreme Leader (yes that’s his title) Kim Jong Un about the size of his “nuclear button.” Then there were the Winter Olympics in South Korea, then there was talk about a meeting between Trump and Kim, then there was a place and a date for that meeting (June 12, 2018, Singapore), then Trump called the whole thing off.

The Democratic People’s Republic of Korea, aka North Korea, describes itself as a self-reliant, socialist state. When the nation invaded South Korea in 1950 it sparked the Korean War.

North Korea is widely viewed as a rogue state and, for more than 40 years, has engaged in a pseudo-militaristic dance with the United States. Recently, questions about North Korea’s increasingly sophisticated cyber attacks have been raised. In 2017, the news was about the steady advances the capital city of Pyongyang made in being able to hit the U.S. with a nuclear weapon. “It won’t happen,” Trump tweeted.

Our question here is: Do people know where North Korea actually is? Let's find out.
