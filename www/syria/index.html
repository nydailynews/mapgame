It’s time to get up to speed on Syria. To recap: In the wake of 2010’s Arab Spring protests, the situation in Syria deteriorated. First, President Bashar al-Assad cracked down on his political adversaries, which spawned a civil war in 2011. Then, in the following seven years, atrocities in the region mounted and the United States began retaliating, first with boots on the ground then with missile attacks.

The latest chemical attack occurred on Saturday in Douma leaving the U.S. to decide on a response. Read a quick recap of Syria’s recent history here, and a timeline of Syria’s chemical weapons use here.
